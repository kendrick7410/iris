# Annotation — Facts & Figures 2025: Growth and Competitiveness

## Phrases d'ouverture caractéristiques

1. "The competitiveness of the European chemical sector remains well below pre-crisis levels (2014-2019 average) driven by weak demand and uncompetitive energy prices." — diagnostic direct, pas d'ancrage chiffré en ouverture, mais le benchmark (2014-2019) est immédiat
2. "This is particularly an issue for high energy consuming, commodity products and petrochemicals, where China holds a competitive edge due to large-scale production and low production costs."
3. "Since March 2022, the EU27 chemicals business environment has been facing a limited demand and declining business confidence, intensified by geopolitical uncertainty."

## Commentaires de graphique

1. "At 74.0% capacity utilisation in the EU27 chemical sector remains a key concern." — précision au dixième, "key concern" = vocabulaire de diagnostic réservé
2. "EU27 chemicals operate at 9.5% below pre-crisis capacity (2014–2019)." — écart vs benchmark exprimé en points de pourcentage
3. "The output of the EU27 chemical industry remains 10% below the pre-crisis levels of 2014 to 2019." — même structure, benchmark en clair

## Tournures de comparaison temporelle

1. "pre-crisis levels (2014-2019 average)" — la comparaison canonique, partout
2. "Since March 2022" — point de rupture temporel récurrent
3. "2022-2025*: Different across Europe" — titre de section avec astérisque pour données préliminaires

## Formulations de nuance / prudence

1. "Unfortunately, no strong positive changes have been observed so far" — "unfortunately" rare dans le registre, marque l'intensité
2. "business expectations for most downstream users are still not encouraging" — double atténuation : "most" + "not encouraging" (litote)

## Paragraphe de conclusion-type

Pas de clôture formelle. Les deux derniers takeaways (emploi + productivité) élargissent vers les conséquences structurelles de la faiblesse.
