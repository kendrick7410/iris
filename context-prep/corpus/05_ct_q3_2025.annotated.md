# Annotation — Chemical Trends Report Q3 2025

## Phrases d'ouverture caractéristiques

1. "The current situation in the chemical industry is worrying." — constat brut, rare pour le registre (le mot "worrying" est plus fort que d'habitude)
2. "Chemical companies are facing a very difficult global economic context." — "very difficult" = calibration forte
3. "The sector continues to face fierce competition, particularly from China." — "fierce competition" = expression réservée à la Chine
4. "The recipes that worked in the past seem to have reached their limits" — métaphore inhabituelle pour le registre, marque un tournant éditorial
5. "Europe has now entered a critical phase." — diagnostic le plus direct du corpus

## Commentaires de graphique

1. "the confidence indicator declined sharply in Germany (-8.5) and France (-7.7), while Belgium saw a modest improvement (+1.9)." — contraste pays par pays, signe + et - explicites
2. "The EU27 chemical industry itself reported an output decrease of 2.5%, far below the overall EU27 manufacturing average." — "far below" + comparaison interne manufacturing
3. "Chemical production trends by country show a fragmented Europe." — phrase de synthèse récurrente, toujours en fin de section pays

## Tournures de comparaison temporelle

1. "in 2025 in comparison to the same period in 2024" — formule canonique du Chemical Trends
2. "in the first eight months of 2025" — fenêtre temporelle YTD, toujours explicite
3. "down by €3.5 bn compared to 2024" — delta absolu + comparatif

## Formulations de nuance / prudence

1. "is not significantly improving" — litote
2. "EU27 chemicals output is expected to decrease by more than 2.0% in 2025" — "is expected to" = hedge standard

## Paragraphe de conclusion-type

Pas de conclusion formelle dans le PDF. La dernière section (trade deficit in volume) se termine par le constat factuel sans transition de clôture. Le rapport se clôt sur les données, pas sur un résumé.
