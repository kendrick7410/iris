## EU27 chemical output remains 18% below pre-crisis levels

EU27 chemical production stood at 79.3 in February 2026 (index 2021=100, seasonally adjusted), down by 3.9% compared to February 2025. The index remains 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average). In the first two months of 2026, chemical production averaged 79.1, down by 4.1% compared to the same period in 2025.

Chemical production underperformed total manufacturing, which declined by 0.4% year-on-year to 100.2 in February 2026. By contrast, motor vehicles production increased by 2.0% to 104.6, while pharmaceuticals decreased by 8.6% to 141.8.

With 100.9, Poland reported the highest chemical production index among major EU27 producers in February 2026, down by 2.1% compared to February 2025. Spain followed at 93.6 (down by 3.3%) and France at 93.5 (up by 1.1%). Germany stood at 77.3 (down by 3.5%), Italy at 81.0 (down by 6.9%), Belgium at 90.6 (down by 1.2%) and the Netherlands at 75.5 (down by 9.6%). These chemical production trends by country show a fragmented Europe.
