## EU27 chemical exports plunge 13.0% in December 2025 amid volume-value divergence

The EU27 chemical industry closed 2025 with mixed signals, as output edged up by 0.9% year-on-year while remaining 16.8% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average). Producer prices declined by 1.3% in December 2025, contributing to a sharp divergence between export volumes and values. EU27 chemical exports rose by 3.0% in volume but fell by 6.0% in value over the year, reflecting lower average unit prices. The trade balance deteriorated by €3.3 bn to €1.09 bn, driven by a 13.0% export decline in December 2025 and a 6.6% import surge over the full year. Germany experienced the steepest output decline at 4.2%, while Poland led growth at 3.1%.

## EU27 chemical output edges up 0.9% in 2025, remaining 16.8% below pre-crisis levels

EU27 chemical production increased by 0.9% in 2025 compared to 2024, reaching an index level of 80.5. The sector remains 16.8% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average), underperforming total manufacturing which grew by 2.4% in 2025.

Poland reported the strongest growth at 3.1%, followed by Spain which remained stable at 0.0%. Germany experienced the steepest decline at 4.2%, followed by Belgium (4.5%) and Italy (3.6%). France and the Netherlands posted modest decreases of 0.4% and 1.9% respectively. These chemical production trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical prices down 1.3% in December 2025

EU27 chemical producer prices decreased by 1.3% in December 2025 compared to December 2024, reaching an index level of 116.6. Over the full year 2025, chemical prices averaged 118.0, down by 0.2% compared to 2024. Chemical prices declined while total manufacturing prices edged up by 0.2%, reflecting sector-specific pressures.

The Netherlands experienced the steepest price decline at 5.2%, followed by Spain (3.1%) and Poland (2.2%). Germany reported a 1.0% decrease, while France posted a modest 0.3% decline. Italy gained 0.5%, and Belgium remained broadly stable with a marginal 0.1% decrease. These chemical price trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical sales decline by 5.7% in 2025

EU27 chemical sales decreased by 5.7% in 2025 compared to 2024, with the index reaching 91.9 in December 2025. The sector underperformed total manufacturing, which grew by 2.1% over the same period.

Belgium experienced the steepest sales decline at 10.8%, followed by the Netherlands (6.9%) and Germany (5.6%). Spain reported the smallest decline at 2.4%, while Poland was the only country to post growth at 0.8%. France declined by 3.4% and Italy by 4.4%.

Chemical sales fell more sharply than other industrial sectors. While pharmaceuticals surged by 9.1% and basic metals climbed by 4.6%, chemicals declined by 5.7% compared to a marginal 1.1% decrease in motor vehicles. These chemical sales trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical exports fell by 13.0% in December 2025

EU27 chemical exports decreased by 13.0% in December 2025 in comparison to December 2024. EU27 chemical exports rose by 3.0% in volume and fell by 6.0% in value between December 2024 and December 2025, reflecting lower average unit prices. The EU27 chemical exports amounted to €288.9 bn in 2025, up by €29.1 bn compared to 2024.

With €2.6 bn, the United States is the primary destination of EU27 chemical exports, followed by the United Kingdom (€1.8 bn) and China (€1.5 bn). The United States experienced the steepest decline at 51.1%, followed by Türkiye (4.2%) and India (3.9%). South Korea surged by 103.3%, while Mexico climbed by 15.5% and Brazil increased by 8.5%.

CN 29 (organic chemicals) fell by 32.0% to €3.0 bn, while CN 28 (inorganic chemicals) dropped by 31.1% to €1.4 bn. CN 30 (pharmaceutical products) rose by 19.2% to €0.7 bn, and CN 31 (fertilisers) increased by 21.8% to €0.5 bn.

## EU27 chemical imports rise by 6.6% in 2025, driven by fertilisers and pharmaceuticals

In 2025, EU27 chemical imports increased by 6.6% in comparison to 2024. The EU27 chemical imports amounted to €230.7 bn in 2025, up by €14.4 bn compared to 2024. EU27 chemical imports rose by 12.5% in volume and 6.6% in value between 2024 and 2025, reflecting a shift toward lower-value categories.

With €3.14 bn, China is the primary source of imports of the EU27 area for chemicals, followed by the United States (€3.08 bn) and Switzerland (€1.69 bn). The United States experienced the steepest increase at 12.1%, followed by India (7.3%) and South Korea (7.3%), while Switzerland posted the largest decline at 19.4%, followed by Japan (10.7%) and the United Kingdom (10.4%). Russia surged by 91.8%, driven by fertilisers and inorganic chemicals following the March 2025 tariff measures.

Fertilisers drove the increase, rising by 271.1% to €1.25 bn, while organic chemicals declined by 4.5% to €6.94 bn. Essential oils and resinoids increased by 12.0% to €1.12 bn, and inorganic chemicals rose by 6.5% to €1.26 bn. Plastics and articles thereof fell by 10.7% to €1.45 bn.
