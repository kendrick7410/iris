## EU27 chemical activity held broadly steady in January 2026 before February's base-effect distortion

The EU27 chemical industry opened 2026 on a mixed but stable footing. Chemical output edged up by 0.6% in January 2026 compared to January 2025, reaching an index level of 78.9, while remaining 18% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average). Producer prices rose by 1.3% to an index of 116.8, reversing part of the 2025 deflation, although the trend turns negative again in February. Chemical sales fell by 7.5% year-on-year, with the index at 90.8, continuing the 2025 trajectory. EU27 chemical exports decreased by 7.9% in value to €15.2 bn, while imports fell by 10.1% to €14.8 bn, bringing the trade balance to €0.4 bn. The month provides a cleaner year-on-year read than February, which is distorted by the February 2025 pre-tariff spike in US-bound flows.

## EU27 chemical output up 0.6% YoY in January 2026, stable against a soft 2025 base

EU27 chemical production increased by 0.6% in January 2026 compared to January 2025, with the index reaching 78.9 (2021=100). The sector remains 18% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average), underperforming total manufacturing which gained 1.2% over the same period.

Chemical production broadly matched total manufacturing, which expanded by 1.2% in January. Pharmaceuticals declined by 4.8%, while basic metals dropped 1.1% and motor vehicles gained 3.2%.

Country trends show continued fragmentation across the EU27. Poland reported the strongest growth at 2.9%, followed by France at 1.4%. Germany posted a marginal decline of 0.3%, while the Netherlands fell by 4.1% and Italy by 2.8%. Spain gained 0.7% and Belgium declined by 1.6%.

## EU27 chemical prices up 1.3% year-on-year in January 2026

EU27 chemical producer prices rose by 1.3% in January 2026 compared to January 2025, reaching an index level of 116.8. The move partially reverses the 2025 deflationary trend, though the February reading returns the series to negative territory.

Chemical prices moved in line with total manufacturing, which gained 1.1% year-on-year. Among industrial sectors, basic metals posted the largest price increase at 7.2%, while pharmaceuticals rose by 1.6% and motor vehicles by 0.8%.

The Netherlands reported the steepest price decline at 4.3%, followed by Belgium (2.1%). Germany gained 2.4% and Spain rose by 1.9%. France posted a small decline of 0.2%, while Italy added 1.1% and Poland gained 0.4%.

## EU27 chemical sales down 7.5% in January 2026

EU27 chemical sales fell by 7.5% in January 2026 compared to January 2025, with the index reaching 90.8 (2021=100). The decline outpaced total manufacturing, which decreased by 1.8% over the same period.

Germany experienced the steepest decline at 8.0%, followed by the Netherlands (14.2%) and Belgium (13.2%). France reported the smallest decrease at 3.1%, while Poland fell by 11.7%. Italy and Spain posted declines of 6.7% and 6.4% respectively.

Among manufacturing sectors, pharmaceuticals recorded the largest drop at 24.4%, while motor vehicles and basic metals gained 4.9% and 4.7% respectively. These chemical sales trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical exports down 7.9% in value in January 2026, ahead of February's anomalous base

EU27 chemical exports decreased by 7.9% in value in January 2026 compared to January 2025. The EU27 chemical exports amounted to €15.2 bn in January 2026, down by €1.3 bn compared to January 2025. In volume terms, exports fell by 3.2%, reflecting a shift toward lower-value categories and mild unit price compression.

Unlike February, January is not distorted by the February 2025 pre-tariff spike in US-bound exports. The 7.9% decline is therefore a cleaner indication of the underlying trend, broadly consistent with the 6.0% full-year 2025 contraction.

Exports to the United States decreased by 11.3% to €2.2 bn, led by organic chemicals and plastics. Exports to the United Kingdom declined by 4.0% to €1.7 bn, while exports to China fell by 6.1% to €1.4 bn. South Korea posted a 9.5% increase, and Brazil gained 4.2%.

Organic chemicals remained the largest export chapter at €3.6 bn, down 8.8% year-on-year. Essential oils and resinoids fell by 2.1% to €1.6 bn, while miscellaneous chemical products declined by 9.0% to €1.4 bn. Plastics and articles thereof dropped by 6.7% to €1.3 bn.

## EU27 chemical imports down 10.1% in value in January 2026

EU27 chemical imports decreased by 10.1% in value in January 2026 compared to January 2025. EU27 chemical imports amounted to €14.8 bn, down by €1.7 bn compared to January 2025. In volume terms, imports fell by 14.8%, reflecting a mild shift toward higher-value categories despite lower average unit prices.

Imports from China declined by 2.1% to €2.0 bn, with polypeptide hormones and other organic basic chemicals accounting for the bulk of the flow. Imports from the United States fell by 8.7% to €1.7 bn, while imports from the United Kingdom dropped by 12.4% to €0.7 bn. Switzerland remained a key source at €0.8 bn, down 5.3%.

Organic chemicals remained the largest import category at €4.2 bn, down 3.6% year-on-year. Plastics and articles thereof fell by 14.9% to €0.8 bn, while miscellaneous chemical products declined by 11.2% to €0.9 bn. Fertilisers continued their upward trajectory, rising by 32.7% to €0.5 bn.
