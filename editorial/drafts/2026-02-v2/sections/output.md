## EU27 chemical output falls 3.9% in February, remaining 18% below pre-crisis levels

EU27 chemical production declined by 3.9% in February 2026 compared to February 2025, with the index reaching 79.3 (2021=100). In the first two months of 2026, chemical output averaged 79.1, down 1.7% compared to the same period in 2025. The sector remains 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average), underscoring the persistent structural weakness since March 2022.

Chemical production underperformed total manufacturing, which declined by only 0.4% in February. Pharmaceuticals fell more, at 8.6%, while basic metals decreased by 0.7% and motor vehicles increased by 2.0%.

The Netherlands experienced the steepest production decline at 9.6%, followed by Italy (6.9%) and Germany (3.5%). Spain decreased by 3.3%, Poland by 2.1%, and Belgium by 1.2%. Only France gained, at 1.1%. These chemical production trends by country show a fragmented Europe.
