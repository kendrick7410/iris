## EU27 chemical exports fell 42.1% in the first two months of 2026

The EU27 chemical industry reported a 42.1% decline in export values during January-February 2026, heavily distorted by an anomalous February 2025 base when pre-tariff front-loading drove exports to €31.6 bn — 4.8 standard deviations above the rolling mean. Output fell 3.9% year-on-year in February, remaining 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average), while producer prices declined 2.8% and the trade balance collapsed to €0.21 bn from €12.42 bn in 2025. Against a normal February 2024 base, exports declined by approximately 10.1%, reflecting underlying structural weakness since March 2022. The Netherlands experienced the steepest production decline at 9.6%, followed by Italy at 6.9%, with only France gaining at 1.1%. Export declines concentrated in bilateral flows to the US (down 72.5%) and Switzerland (down 75.6%), with 2 partners accounting for 95.2% of the absolute variation.

## EU27 chemical output falls 3.9% in February, remaining 18% below pre-crisis levels

EU27 chemical production declined by 3.9% in February 2026 compared to February 2025, with the index reaching 79.3 (2021=100). In the first two months of 2026, chemical output averaged 79.1, down 1.7% compared to the same period in 2025. The sector remains 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average), underscoring the persistent structural weakness since March 2022.

Chemical production underperformed total manufacturing, which declined by only 0.4% in February. Pharmaceuticals fell more, at 8.6%, while basic metals decreased by 0.7% and motor vehicles increased by 2.0%.

The Netherlands experienced the steepest production decline at 9.6%, followed by Italy (6.9%) and Germany (3.5%). Spain decreased by 3.3%, Poland by 2.1%, and Belgium by 1.2%. Only France gained, at 1.1%. These chemical production trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical prices decline 2.8% year-on-year in February 2026

EU27 chemical producer prices declined by 2.8% in February 2026 compared to February 2025, with the index reaching 117.2 (2021=100). In the first two months of 2026, chemical prices averaged 117.0, down 0.8% compared to the same period in 2025.

Chemical prices fell more sharply than total manufacturing, which edged up by 0.2% year-on-year in February. Pharmaceuticals rose by 1.9%, while basic metals surged by 8.8% and motor vehicles increased by 0.9%.

The Netherlands experienced the steepest price decline at 9.2%, followed by Belgium (5.5%) and France (3.0%). Germany reported a 1.7% decrease, Spain fell by 2.8%, and Poland declined by 1.9%. Italy showed the smallest decrease at 0.3%.

## EU27 chemical sales index falls 7.5% in January 2026

EU27 chemical sales declined by 7.5% in January 2026 compared to January 2025, with the index reaching 90.8 (2021=100). The sector underperformed total manufacturing, which fell by 1.8% over the same period.

Germany experienced the steepest decline at 8.0%, followed by the Netherlands (14.2%) and Belgium (13.2%). Poland fell by 11.7%, Spain by 6.4%, and Italy by 6.7%. France reported the smallest decline at 3.1%. These chemical sales trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

Among manufacturing sectors, pharmaceuticals fell more sharply at 24.4%, while basic metals and motor vehicles posted gains of 4.7% and 4.9% respectively.

## EU27 chemical exports fell 42.1% in value in the first two months of 2026 — a figure heavily influenced by an anomalous February 2025 base — reflecting concentrated declines in bilateral flows to the US and Switzerland

From January to February 2026, EU27 chemical exports decreased by 42.1% in value compared to the same period in 2025. EU27 chemical exports fell by 17.4% in volume and 42.1% in value between January-February 2025 and January-February 2026, reflecting a shift toward lower-value categories and unit price compression.

This figure reflects an unusually elevated base in February 2025, driven by pre-tariff front-loading ahead of US trade measures. EU27 exports averaged €22.3 bn/month in the 12 months preceding February 2025 but spiked to €31.6 bn in February 2025 — 4.8 standard deviations above the rolling mean. Against the February 2024 base (€20.2 bn), exports are down by approximately 10.1%, a more representative indication of the underlying trend.

With €3.6 bn, the United States is the primary destination of EU27 chemical exports in February 2026, followed by the United Kingdom (€2.1 bn) and China (€1.4 bn). In February alone, exports to the US were down 72.5% and exports to Switzerland fell 75.6%, while the United Kingdom gained 4.9%. With 2 partners accounting for 95.2% of the absolute variation in export values, the decline is concentrated in a narrow set of bilateral flows.

CN 33 (essential oils and resinoids) reported the smallest decline at 8.4%, followed by CN 38 (miscellaneous chemical products) at 5.1% and CN 32 (tanning or dyeing extracts) at 5.3%. CN 29 (organic chemicals) plummeted by 77.5%, while CN 27 (mineral fuels) fell 57.0%.

## EU27 chemical imports down 12.0% in value in first two months of 2026

From January to February 2026, EU27 chemical imports decreased by 12.0% in value in comparison to the same period in 2025. EU27 chemical imports fell by 18.5% in volume and 12.0% in value between January and February 2026, reflecting a shift toward higher-value categories. The EU27 chemical imports amounted to €33.5 bn in the first two months of 2026, down by €4.6 bn compared to 2025.

With €4.4 bn, China is the primary source of imports of the EU27 area for chemicals, followed by the United States (€3.7 bn) and Switzerland (€1.9 bn). China increased by 4.8% while the United States grew by 4.7%, whereas Switzerland declined by 19.9%. South Korea experienced the steepest decline at 30.6%, followed by Japan (30.6%) and South Korea (29.1%). Singapore posted the largest gain at 44.0%, followed by China (4.8%) and the United States (4.7%).
