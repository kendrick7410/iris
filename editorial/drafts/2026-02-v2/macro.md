## EU27 chemical exports fell 42.1% in the first two months of 2026

The EU27 chemical industry reported a 42.1% decline in export values during January-February 2026, heavily distorted by an anomalous February 2025 base when pre-tariff front-loading drove exports to €31.6 bn — 4.8 standard deviations above the rolling mean. Output fell 3.9% year-on-year in February, remaining 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average), while producer prices declined 2.8% and the trade balance collapsed to €0.21 bn from €12.42 bn in 2025. Against a normal February 2024 base, exports declined by approximately 10.1%, reflecting underlying structural weakness since March 2022. The Netherlands experienced the steepest production decline at 9.6%, followed by Italy at 6.9%, with only France gaining at 1.1%.
