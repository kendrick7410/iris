## The EU27 chemical industry faces deepening contraction amid fragmented recovery

- Chemical production declined by 3.9% in February 2026, reaching an index of 79.3 and remaining 18.0% below pre-crisis levels. The sector significantly underperformed total manufacturing, which fell only 0.4%.

- Producer prices decreased by 2.8% in February 2026 to 117.2, contrasting with manufacturing's 0.2% increase. The Netherlands led price declines at 9.2%, while basic metals surged 8.8%, highlighting competitive pressure on chemicals.

- Geographic fragmentation persists with France posting the only production increase at 1.1%, while the Netherlands suffered a 9.6% decline. Sales indices vary dramatically from Poland's 104.6 to the Netherlands' 79.0.

- The industry's structural challenges intensify as chemical output averaged 79.1 in early 2026, down 1.5% year-on-year, whilst other sectors like motor vehicles managed 2.0% growth.

## EU27 chemical output down 3.9% in February 2026

EU27 chemical production declined by 3.9% in February 2026 compared to February 2025, with the index reaching 79.3. The sector remains 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average). In the first two months of 2026, chemical output averaged 79.1, down 1.5% compared to the same period in 2025.

Chemical production underperformed total manufacturing, which decreased by 0.4% in February 2026. Pharmaceuticals fell more, at 8.6%, while motor vehicles increased by 2.0% and basic metals declined by 0.7%.

France reported the only increase at 1.1%, followed by Belgium with the smallest decline at 1.2%. The Netherlands experienced the steepest decline at 9.6%, followed by Italy (6.9%) and Germany (3.5%). Spain and Poland posted decreases of 3.3% and 2.1% respectively. These chemical production trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical prices decline by 2.8% in February 2026

EU27 chemical producer prices decreased by 2.8% in February 2026 compared to February 2025, reaching an index level of 117.2. In the first two months of 2026, chemical prices averaged 117.0, down by 0.7% compared to the same period in 2025.

Chemical prices declined more than total manufacturing, which edged up by 0.2% in February 2026. By contrast, basic metals prices surged by 8.8%, while pharmaceuticals increased by 1.9% and motor vehicles by 0.9%.

The Netherlands experienced the steepest price decline at 9.2%, followed by Belgium (5.5%) and France (3.0%). Germany reported a decrease of 1.7%, Spain fell by 2.8%, and Poland declined by 1.9%. Italy showed the smallest decline at 0.3%. These chemical price trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical sales index at 90.8 in January 2026

EU27 chemical sales reached an index level of 90.8 in January 2026 (2021=100, seasonally adjusted). The sector underperformed total manufacturing, which recorded 123.2, and pharmaceuticals at 123.2.

Poland reported the highest chemical sales index at 104.6, followed by Spain (98.1) and Italy (94.2). France recorded 101.3, while Germany stood at 89.3, Belgium at 88.5, and the Netherlands at 79.0.
