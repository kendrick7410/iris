## EU27 chemical exports fall 42% in February, distorted by anomalous 2025 base

The EU27 chemical industry reported a 42.1% decline in export value in the first two months of 2026, heavily influenced by pre-tariff front-loading in February 2025 that created an abnormal comparison base. Chemical output fell 3.9% year-on-year in February, reaching 79.3 on the 2021 index, while remaining 18% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average). Producer prices declined 2.8% year-on-year, reflecting persistent deflationary pressures across the sector. The trade balance collapsed to €0.2 bn from €12.4 bn in early 2025, driven by the export contraction and resilient import flows from China (+4.8%) and the United States (+4.7%). With the top-5 export partners accounting for 50% of value, trade flows remain concentrated despite the structural disruption, while volume exports fell less sharply at 17.4%, indicating a shift toward lower-value product categories.

## EU27 chemical output down 3.9% in February, 18% below pre-crisis levels

EU27 chemical production declined by 3.9% in February 2026 compared to February 2025, with the index reaching 79.3 (2021=100). In the first two months of 2026, chemical output averaged 79.1, down 1.7% compared to the same period in 2025. The sector remains 18% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average).

Chemical production underperformed total manufacturing, which declined by just 0.4% in February. Pharmaceuticals fell more, at 8.6%, while basic metals dropped 0.7% and motor vehicles gained 2.0%.

Country trends show significant variation across the EU27. The Netherlands experienced the steepest decline at 9.6%, followed by Italy (6.9%) and Germany (3.5%). Spain fell 3.3%, Poland declined 2.1%, and Belgium dropped 1.2%. Only France gained, at 1.1%.

## EU27 chemical prices down 2.8% year-on-year in February 2026

EU27 chemical producer prices declined by 2.8% in February 2026 compared to February 2025, reaching an index level of 117.2. In the first two months of 2026, chemical prices averaged 117.0, down 0.8% compared to the same period in 2025.

Chemical prices fell more sharply than total manufacturing, which remained broadly stable at 0.2% year-on-year. Among industrial sectors, basic metals experienced the steepest price increases at 8.8%, while pharmaceuticals rose by 1.9% and motor vehicles by 0.9%.

The Netherlands experienced the steepest price decline at 9.2%, followed by Belgium (5.5%) and France (3.0%). Germany reported a 1.7% decrease, while Spain matched the EU27 average at 2.8%. Italy posted the smallest decline at 0.3%. Poland decreased by 1.9%.

## EU27 chemical sales down 7.5% in January 2026

EU27 chemical sales fell by 7.5% in January 2026 compared to January 2025, with the index reaching 90.8 (2021=100). The decline outpaced total manufacturing, which decreased by 1.8% over the same period.

Germany experienced the steepest decline at 8.0%, followed by the Netherlands (14.2%) and Belgium (13.2%). France reported the smallest decrease at 3.1%, while Poland fell by 11.7%. Italy and Spain posted declines of 6.7% and 6.4% respectively.

Among manufacturing sectors, pharmaceuticals recorded the largest drop at 24.4%, while motor vehicles and basic metals gained 4.9% and 4.7% respectively. These chemical sales trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical exports fall 42% in February, distorted by anomalous 2025 base

From January to February 2026, EU27 chemical exports decreased by 42.1% in value in comparison to the same period in 2025, a figure heavily influenced by an anomalous February 2025 base. Exports amounted to €34.5 bn in the first two months of 2026, down by €25.1 bn compared to 2025.

This figure reflects an unusually high base in February 2025, driven by pre-tariff front-loading ahead of US trade measures. EU exports to the US averaged €22.3 bn per month in the preceding 12 months but spiked to €31.6 bn in February 2025, 4.8 standard deviations above the rolling mean. Against a normal base (February 2024), exports are down by approximately 10%, a more representative indication of the underlying trend.

EU27 chemical exports fell by 17.4% in volume and 42.1% in value between January-February 2025 and January-February 2026, reflecting a shift toward lower-value categories and unit price compression.

Exports to the United States decreased by €9.4 bn (72.5%), driven by 29371900 (heterocyclic compounds) (€8.2 bn decline) and 29335995 (heterocyclic compounds, pyrimidine/piperazine ring) (€0.4 bn decline). The largest current flow remains 29349990 (heterocyclic compounds) (€0.5 bn, 13% of the total).

Exports to the United Kingdom increased by €0.1 bn (4.9%), driven by 28442035 (inorganic compounds) (€0.2 bn gain) and 29335995 (heterocyclic compounds, pyrimidine/piperazine ring) (€0.03 bn gain). The largest current flow remains 28442035 (inorganic compounds) (€0.3 bn, 13% of the total).

Exports to China decreased by €0.1 bn (7.3%), driven by 38180010 (chemical elements and compounds) (€0.04 bn decline) and 38221900 (diagnostic or laboratory reagents) (€0.02 bn decline). The largest current flow remains 33049900 (beauty or make-up preparations) (€0.1 bn, 10% of the total).

With €3.5 bn, organic chemicals is the largest export chapter of the EU27 area for chemicals, followed by essential oils and resinoids (€3.6 bn) and miscellaneous chemical products (€3.1 bn). Organic chemicals fell 77.5% compared to February 2025, while inorganic chemicals gained 25%.

## EU27 chemical imports fell 12% in value in the first two months of 2026

From January to February 2026, EU27 chemical imports decreased by 12.0% in value in comparison to the same period in 2025. EU27 chemical imports amounted to €33.5 bn in the first two months of 2026, down by €4.6 bn compared to 2025. In volume terms, imports fell by 18.5%, reflecting a shift toward higher-value categories despite lower average unit prices.

Imports from China increased by 4.8% to €4.4 bn in February 2026, driven by polypeptide hormones (€0.9 bn) and other organic basic chemicals (€1.5 bn). Basic pharmaceutical products accounted for 41% of Chinese imports, followed by other organic basic chemicals at 35%. The largest current flow remains polypeptide hormones (€0.9 bn, 20% of the total).

Imports from the United States rose by 4.7% to €3.7 bn, led by polypeptide hormones (€1.5 bn) and diagnostic or laboratory reagents (€0.3 bn). Basic pharmaceutical products represented 46% of US imports, with other organic basic chemicals at 18%. Polypeptide hormones alone explain 439% of the net variation in US imports.

Imports from the United Kingdom declined by 13.3% to €1.5 bn in February 2026, driven by mineral fuels (down €0.1 bn) and polyethylene products (down €0.0 bn). The flow is more diversified than other key partners, with other organic basic chemicals, other chemical products, and other inorganic basic chemicals each representing 16% of UK imports.

With 5 CN chapters accounting for 73% of imports, the product base shows moderate concentration. Organic chemicals remained the largest import category at €9.2 bn, virtually unchanged from 2025 (down 0.3%). Miscellaneous chemical products fell by 9.1% to €1.8 bn, while plastics and articles thereof dropped by 18.4% to €1.6 bn.
