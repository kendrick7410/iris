## EU27 chemical exports fall by 42.1% in the first two months of 2026

From January to February 2026, EU27 chemical exports decreased by 42.1% in value and 17.4% in volume in comparison to the same period in 2025. The EU27 chemical exports amounted to €34.5 bn in the first two months of 2026, down by €25.1 bn compared to 2025.

The decline was driven by sharp falls in exports to key trading partners. With €3.56 bn, the United States is the primary export destination of the EU27 area for chemicals, followed by the United Kingdom (€2.13 bn) and China (€1.40 bn). The United States experienced the steepest decline at 72.5%, followed by Switzerland (75.6%) and Japan (15.9%). By contrast, the United Kingdom increased by 4.9%, South Korea by 11.0% and Brazil by 5.3%.

Organic chemicals fell by 77.5%, reflecting the largest absolute decline among product categories. Essential oils and resinoids decreased by 8.4%, while miscellaneous chemical products declined by 5.1%. Inorganic chemicals increased by 25.0%, the only major category to post growth.

These chemical export trends by country show a fragmented Europe.
