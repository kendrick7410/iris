## EU27 chemical exports collapse 42.1% as trade balance turns negative in February 2026

The EU27 chemical industry reported a sharp contraction in the first two months of 2026, with output declining by 3.9% year-on-year to 79.3 and remaining 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average). Exports collapsed by 42.1% in value while falling only 17.4% in volume, reflecting a severe compression in average unit prices that left the trade balance at just €0.21 bn compared to €12.42 bn in 2025. With 5 export partners accounting for 50.0% of trade value, concentration remained high despite the United States experiencing the steepest decline at 72.5% and Switzerland falling by 75.6%. Producer prices declined by 2.8% in February 2026, underscoring deflationary pressures across the sector.

## EU27 chemical output remains 18.0% below pre-crisis levels in February 2026

EU27 chemical production stood at 79.3 in February 2026, down by 3.9% compared to February 2025. In the first two months of 2026, chemical output averaged 79.1, declining by 1.7% compared to the same period in 2025.

The sector remains 18.0% below pre-crisis levels (2014–2019 average). Chemical production underperformed total manufacturing, which declined by 0.4% year-on-year in February 2026, while pharmaceuticals fell more, at 8.6%.

## EU27 chemical prices decline 2.8% in February 2026

EU27 chemical producer prices stood at 117.2 in February 2026, down by 2.8% compared to February 2025. In the first two months of 2026, chemical prices averaged 117.0, down by 0.8% compared to the same period in 2025.

Chemical prices declined while total manufacturing prices remained broadly stable at 0.2%. Pharmaceuticals bucked the trend with prices rising by 1.9%, while basic metals experienced the steepest increase at 8.8%.

## EU27 chemical exports fall by 42.1% in the first two months of 2026

From January to February 2026, EU27 chemical exports decreased by 42.1% in value and 17.4% in volume in comparison to the same period in 2025. The EU27 chemical exports amounted to €34.5 bn in the first two months of 2026, down by €25.1 bn compared to 2025.

The decline was driven by sharp falls in exports to key trading partners. With €3.56 bn, the United States is the primary export destination of the EU27 area for chemicals, followed by the United Kingdom (€2.13 bn) and China (€1.40 bn). The United States experienced the steepest decline at 72.5%, followed by Switzerland (75.6%) and Japan (15.9%). By contrast, the United Kingdom increased by 4.9%, South Korea by 11.0% and Brazil by 5.3%.

Organic chemicals fell by 77.5%, reflecting the largest absolute decline among product categories. Essential oils and resinoids decreased by 8.4%, while miscellaneous chemical products declined by 5.1%. Inorganic chemicals increased by 25.0%, the only major category to post growth.

These chemical export trends by country show a fragmented Europe.

## EU27 chemical imports fell by 12.0% in the first two months of 2026

From January to February 2026, EU27 chemical imports decreased by 12.0% in comparison to the same period in 2025. EU27 chemical imports fell by 18.5% in volume and 12.0% in value between January and February 2025 and 2026, reflecting lower average unit prices. The EU27 chemical imports amounted to €33.5 bn in the first two months of 2026, down by €4.6 bn compared to 2025.

With €4.4 bn, China is the primary source of imports of the EU27 area for chemicals, followed by the United States (€3.7 bn) and Switzerland (€1.9 bn). China increased by 4.8% while the United States rose by 4.7%. Switzerland experienced the steepest decline at 19.9%, followed by South Korea (29.1%) and Japan (30.6%). Singapore posted the largest gain at 44.0%.

With 5 CN 8-digit codes accounting for 64.0% of the change in Chinese imports between February 2021 and February 2026: 29371900 (polypeptide hormones), +€0.9 bn; 29339980 (heterocyclic compounds with nitrogen…), +€0.6 bn; 29335995 (heterocyclic compounds with nitrogen…), +€0.5 bn; 29349990 (nucleic acids and their salts), +€0.1 bn; 24041200 (products containing nicotine…), +€0.1 bn.

Organic chemicals remained the largest import category at €9.2 bn, edging down by 0.3%. Miscellaneous chemical products declined by 9.1% to €1.8 bn, while plastics and articles thereof fell by 18.4% to €1.6 bn. Fertilisers dropped sharply by 47.2% to €0.4 bn.
