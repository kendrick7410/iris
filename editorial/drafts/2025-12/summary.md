## The EU27 chemical industry faces mounting competitive pressures despite modest output recovery

- Chemical production edged up 0.9% in 2025 but remains 16.8% below pre-crisis levels, underperforming total manufacturing which grew by 2.4%.
- Producer prices declined by 1.3% in December 2025 while imports surged 6.6% for the full year, with volumes climbing 12.5% and reflecting lower unit costs from overseas suppliers.
- China maintained its position as the largest import source at €75.3 bn despite a 6.8% decline, while fertiliser imports from Russia more than doubled at 91.8% following supply chain disruptions.
- Chemical sales fell 5.7% in December 2025 and averaged 3.6% lower for the full year, with exports declining 13.0% in December despite an 11.2% annual increase driven by higher unit values.
