## EU27 chemical exports fell by 13.0% in December 2025

EU27 chemical exports decreased by 13.0% in December 2025 in comparison to December 2024. EU27 chemical exports rose by 3.0% in volume and fell by 6.0% in value between December 2024 and December 2025, reflecting lower average unit prices. The EU27 chemical exports amounted to €288.9 bn in 2025, up by €29.1 bn compared to 2024.

With €2.6 bn, the United States is the primary destination of EU27 chemical exports, followed by the United Kingdom (€1.8 bn) and China (€1.5 bn). The United States experienced the steepest decline at 51.1%, followed by Türkiye (4.2%) and India (3.9%). South Korea surged by 103.3%, while Mexico climbed by 15.5% and Brazil increased by 8.5%.

CN 29 (organic chemicals) fell by 32.0% to €3.0 bn, while CN 28 (inorganic chemicals) dropped by 31.1% to €1.4 bn. CN 30 (pharmaceutical products) rose by 19.2% to €0.7 bn, and CN 31 (fertilisers) increased by 21.8% to €0.5 bn.
