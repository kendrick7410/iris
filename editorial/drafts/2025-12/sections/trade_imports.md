## EU27 chemical imports rise by 6.6% in 2025, driven by fertilisers and pharmaceuticals

In 2025, EU27 chemical imports increased by 6.6% in comparison to 2024. The EU27 chemical imports amounted to €230.7 bn in 2025, up by €14.4 bn compared to 2024. EU27 chemical imports rose by 12.5% in volume and 6.6% in value between 2024 and 2025, reflecting a shift toward lower-value categories.

With €3.14 bn, China is the primary source of imports of the EU27 area for chemicals, followed by the United States (€3.08 bn) and Switzerland (€1.69 bn). The United States experienced the steepest increase at 12.1%, followed by India (7.3%) and South Korea (7.3%), while Switzerland posted the largest decline at 19.4%, followed by Japan (10.7%) and the United Kingdom (10.4%). Russia surged by 91.8%, driven by fertilisers and inorganic chemicals following the March 2025 tariff measures.

Fertilisers drove the increase, rising by 271.1% to €1.25 bn, while organic chemicals declined by 4.5% to €6.94 bn. Essential oils and resinoids increased by 12.0% to €1.12 bn, and inorganic chemicals rose by 6.5% to €1.26 bn. Plastics and articles thereof fell by 10.7% to €1.45 bn.
